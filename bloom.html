Bloom Sex Education

Brand Identity, Illustration, Web Design, App Design

Bloom Sex Education is a company I created for my Thesis project at Niagara College. I spent several months creating Bloom; an alternate sex education platform free for 13-19 year old youth in Canada.

To address the problem of lack of inclusive, comprehensive sex education provided by the government in Canada, I created this alternate sex education learning platform to make LGBTQ+ youth feel included, represented, and give them the expert knowledge they need to be safe, healthy and informed. The understanding of identity, gender, and sex, with the promotion of mental health and well-being could lower health risks to LGBTQ+ youth.

Project Goals

  • Goal 1: Give every child the opportunity to exercise their right to comprehensive sex education by providing a mobile learning platform.
  • Goal 2: Provide expert sex education resources and create a safe community space within the platform that will build confidence in Canadian youth.
  • Goal 3: Create a tailored learning experience that is anonymous and engaging with interactive lessons that appeal to the Gen Z target audience.

Logo Meaning

The logo symbol is a minimal, modern line logo mark. The three curved lines represent half of a rainbow, representing diversity and LGBTQ+ inclusivity. They rotate parallel to each other to show that each unique individual is in this together as a community. They also represent the motion of the line heart to the right.

The heart represents care and love that the brand embodies. It also symbolizes that sex education is not just about sex, but rather the importance of healthy relationships between anyone. Since the heart is tilted on its side, it also forms a B for “Bloom”.

The illustration style is inspired by corporate flat character design but designed to have a more sketched, illustrated feel. The variation in brush strokes gives that stylistic difference to the typical flat design. The characters have more detail and attention to their clothing so that it appeals to the Gen Z audience and makes each character truly unique. Body proportions are relatively accurate to real people so they are easily identifiable as teenagers (the target audience).
